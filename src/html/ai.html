AI × Austin Glander

A concise, professional overview of Austin and a live demonstration of what modern AI can generate—using only clean HTML.

The Prompt (verbatim)

I need you to generate an engaging webpage that educates the user on me, Austin Glander. It should be relevant to me and my interests, professional, and based off of all of our prior engagements. Your ultimate goal is to present the capabilities of artificial intelligence for generating web content. This prompt should be included in its unaltered entirety within the page. You must generate ONLY html. That means no inline CSS or JavaScript. You may include external links if desired. You must include a navbar at the top of the page that links to index.html, portfolio.html, qualifications.html, service.html, and ai.html (you are writing ai.html itself).

About Austin

Austin Glander is an educator-builder focused on computer networking, cybersecurity, and practical software. He leads networking education efforts at Texas A&M and designs hands-on labs aligned to CCNA/CCNP concepts. Outside the classroom, he experiments with a serious homelab, automates infrastructure with containers and overlay networks, and builds tools and games that make technical learning more approachable.

  • Teaching & Leadership: Designs Layer-2 first curricula, runs lecture-plus-lab sessions, and iterates based on student feedback.
  • Networking Focus: VLANs, STP/LoopGuard, EtherChannels, OSPF/BGP fundamentals, NAT design, IPv6, and operational reliability.
  • Homelab & Infra: Dockerized services, reverse proxying, DNS filtering, and secure remote access with mesh VPN.
  • Software Projects: JavaFX apps, Python bots, and network-automation scripts; growing interest in modern frontend with React.
  • Community & Style: Clear, neutral tone; emphasis on authenticity, correctness, and reproducible how-tos.

What Austin Builds & Learns

Networking Education

Short, conceptual primers followed by guided labs (often in Packet Tracer or on real Cisco gear). Students practice VLAN planning, trunking, Rapid PVST, and Link Aggregation; later, they layer on routing, ACLs, and IPv6.

External references: Cisco Networking Academy, CCNA exam overview

Homelab & Reliability

Multi-container services behind a reverse proxy, internal DNS, and clean network segmentation. Experiments with 4×4 vehicles and portable edge nodes occasionally meet a love of field networking and telemetry.

External references: Docker, Tailscale

Software & Teaching Tools

From a JavaFX “Foodtruck” app to Discord bots and subnetting games, Austin builds playful utilities that teach real skills. Current interests include React-based learning tools and visualizers for routing and binary arithmetic.

External references: React, VS Code

Curiosity for Internet Operations

Deep dives into historical routing incidents and the practical tradeoffs of anycast, peering policies, and router safeguards—bridging theory and real-world operations.

What AI Can Generate for Web Content

This page itself was produced from the prompt above and prior context, with no CSS or JavaScript. Here are patterns AI can deliver quickly while staying aligned to your voice and goals:

  • Audience-aware structure: Clear headings, summaries, and “why it matters” callouts for learners vs. practitioners.
  • Reusable sections: “About,” “Focus Areas,” and “Capabilities” blocks that port across your site.
  • Documentation-ready markup: Lists, figures, and semantic HTML that can be styled later without rewrites.
  • Pedagogical scaffolding: Stepwise lab plans, checklists, and assessment prompts tailored to CCNA learners.
  • Tone & precision control: Neutral, concise copy with concrete terms favored by technical audiences.
  • Rapid iteration: Update a single prompt, regenerate sections, and keep the site consistent.
Example: From outline to publish-ready HTML
  1. Gather constraints (no CSS/JS, navbar targets, audience, voice).
  2. Assemble a content map (About, Interests, Labs, AI capabilities).
  3. Render clean, accessible HTML with semantic tags.
  4. Link to internal pages and reputable external references.
  5. Iterate on sections without changing the overall structure.
Ethics, Safety, and Accuracy
  • Prefer verifiable, non-sensitive facts and avoid over-claiming.
  • Keep user privacy in mind; share only what’s relevant and consented.
  • Invite review: AI drafts fast; humans keep it correct and authentic.

Teaching Philosophy

Lead with intuition, then formalize. Show the “why,” then the CLI. Make labs bite-sized and confidence-building, with optional depth for advanced students. Encourage note-taking, self-tests, and post-lab retros.

  • Concept → Command → Consequence: Understand, configure, verify.
  • Minimal friction: Known-good baselines and clear rollback steps.
  • Realistic failure: Use safe, instructive breakages to build instincts.
